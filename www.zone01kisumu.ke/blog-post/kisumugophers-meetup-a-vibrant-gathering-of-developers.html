Kisumu Gophers MeetUp: A Vibrant Gathering of Developers

Written By:
Joab Owala
Published On:
June 3, 2024
Share

The recent Gophers Kisumu MeetUp was a vibrant and enlightening event, drawing over 160 sign ups and more than 80 in-person attendees. Held on the 11th of May 2024 at Zone01 Kisumu at the Lake Basin Mall, this gathering brought together young and upcoming developers all eager to learn, share, and connect on everything #Go.¬†

The highlight of the event was the keynote address by Sigu Magwa, veteran community builder, tech speaker and software engineer. He covered a range of topics, including open source contributions and technical debates, providing valuable insights to all gophers in attendance.

Paul Oguda, Head of Tech at Zone01 Kisumu and one of the speakers emphasized the importance of such communities. He shared his personal journey, explaining how being part of a community like Elixir helped him secure his first job. This narrative underscored the critical role that professional networks play in career development.

The significance of contributing to open-source projects was another focal point of the event. Participants discussed how engaging with open-source software not only enhances individual skills but also fosters a collaborative spirit within the developer community. Discussions extended into the realm of artificial intelligence (AI).¬†

Attendees explored how AI is shaping the future of software development and strategized on how to adopt winning strategies in this rapidly evolving field.

The event also addressed the common challenges faced by new developers. A recurring theme was the difficulty of juggling multiple technologies. Attendees debated the merits of mastering one programming language before moving on to others, versus the benefits of diversifying their technical skillset early on.¬†

Career pathways were another key discussion point, particularly the pros and cons of freelancing versus traditional employment. The session provided practical advice on setting freelancing rates and navigating the job market, aiming to equip developers with the knowledge to make informed career decisions.

Imposter syndrome, a common issue in tech communities, was also addressed. Attendees shared their experiences and coping strategies, emphasizing the supportive nature of developer communities in overcoming such challenges. The event concluded with technical debates, including the ever-relevant discussion on the use of short versus long functions in coding. These debates not only highlighted the diverse opinions within the community but also served as a learning platform for all participants.

To wrap up, the Kisumu Gophers MeetUp was more than just a gathering; it was a melting pot of ideas, experiences, and aspirations. Events like these are crucial for fostering a strong, connected, and innovative developer community. As the largest gathering of pioneering Go developers in Kenya, this meet-up marked a significant milestone in the tech landscape of Kisumu, promising more such vibrant interactions in the future.
